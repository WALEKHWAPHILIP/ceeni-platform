2. Summary of Key Themes

The Constitution of Kenya, 2010 is built on principles that reflect the nation’s deepest aspirations. Below are some of the key features every citizen should understand:

  • Devolution of Power: Authority is shared between the national and 47 county governments, bringing services, accountability, and leadership closer to the people.
  • Bill of Rights: A comprehensive set of rights and freedoms that protect every individual regardless of ethnicity, gender, religion, or background — enshrined in Chapter Four.
  • Leadership and Integrity: Chapter Six sets high moral and ethical standards for all public officers to combat corruption and promote accountability.
  • Inclusivity and Equity: The Constitution demands representation, fairness, and the protection of marginalized groups — laying the foundation for ethnic and gender inclusion in public institutions.
  • Participatory Democracy: Kenyans have the right to participate in decision-making processes at all levels — through voting, petitions, referenda, and public consultation.
