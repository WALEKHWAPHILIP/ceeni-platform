9. Second Schedule — Equity Scorecard Metrics and Grading Scale

(Section 19(2))

Core Metrics

(1) The Equity Scorecard shall evaluate public institutions using the following core metrics—

(a) Representation Ratio — Comparison of an institution’s ethnic composition to national demographics (based on the most recent census and calculated in accordance with the Fifth Schedule);

(b) Leadership Equity — Representation of ethnic groups in leadership and decision-making roles, disaggregated by gender and disability;

(c) Recruitment Equity — Diversity in new hires and promotions within the reporting year;

(d) Service Delivery Equity — Geographic and demographic reach of services relative to the population served;

(e) Trend Improvement — Year-on-year progress in metrics (a) to (d);

(f) Transparency — Timeliness, completeness, and accuracy of data submissions, including responsiveness to audit queries.

Grading Scale

(2) The following grades shall be used to classify institutional performance—

Grade Description
AExceeds benchmarks in all metrics. Demonstrates sustained improvement and proactive diversity measures.
BMeets or exceeds benchmarks in most metrics; minor, correctable shortfalls in others.
CMeets minimum legal thresholds but shows no measurable improvement.
DFalls below minimum legal thresholds in multiple metrics; significant improvement required.
EPersistent non-compliance or failure to submit required data.

Application Notes

(3) The application of this Schedule shall be guided by the following—

(a) The Commission shall publish the benchmark thresholds and grading criteria annually in the Gazette;

(b) Institutions graded D or E shall be subject to remedial action plans and follow-up audits within six months;

(c) Performance against these metrics shall be published on the KPEID Dashboard and tabled in Parliament annually.
