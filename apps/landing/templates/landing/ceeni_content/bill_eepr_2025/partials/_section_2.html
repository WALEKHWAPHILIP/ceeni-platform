2. Part II — Principles and Objectives

Guiding principles.

The implementation of this Act shall be guided by the following principles —

(a) equality, non-discrimination, and inclusiveness as guaranteed under Article 27 of the Constitution;

(b) recognition of the unique needs of marginalized and minority communities as per Article 56 of the Constitution;

(c) national unity and cohesion as fundamental values under Article 10 of the Constitution;

(d) merit, transparency, and fairness in public appointments and decision-making;

(e) the right of every Kenyan to access public service without ethnic bias or exclusion;

(f) accountability and measurable compliance with inclusion standards;

(g) proportional representation that reflects the ethnic diversity of the Kenyan population;

(h) timely and accurate public reporting on representation data and benchmarks;

(i) public participation and civic engagement in matters of inclusion and fairness.

Objectives of the Act.

The objectives of this Act are to —

(a) give effect to Articles 10, 27, 56, and 232 of the Constitution concerning equity, representation, and non-discrimination in public service;

(b) promote inclusive governance by ensuring that all ethnic communities are fairly represented in public institutions and leadership positions;

(c) establish legal mechanisms for the enforcement, oversight, and reporting of ethnic equity standards in the public sector;

(d) create and empower the Commission for Ethnic Equity and National Inclusion to monitor, evaluate, and enforce compliance with this Act;

(e) ensure that data on public service composition is collected, verified, and made accessible to all Kenyans;

(f) provide a framework for complaints, redress, and sanctions in cases of exclusion, underrepresentation, or ethnic bias;

(g) affirm the dignity of every Kenyan by institutionalizing fairness as a national value.
