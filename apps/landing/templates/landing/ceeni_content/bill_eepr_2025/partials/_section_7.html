7. Part VII — Miscellaneous Provisions

Protection from personal liability.

No member, officer, or employee of the Commission shall be held personally liable for any act or omission done in good faith in the execution of the functions of the Commission under this Act.

Offences and penalties.

(1) Any person who —

(a) knowingly provides false information to the Commission;

(b) obstructs or hinders the Commission in the discharge of its duties; or

(c) fails to comply with a lawful directive issued under this Act,

commits an offence and shall be liable, upon conviction, to a fine not exceeding one million shillings or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding two years, or to both.

(2) Where an offence is committed by a public institution, the Commission may recommend appropriate administrative or disciplinary action against responsible officers.

Regulations.

(1) The Cabinet Secretary may, in consultation with the Commission, make regulations generally for the better carrying out of the provisions of this Act.

(2) Without prejudice to the generality of subsection (1), such regulations may provide for —

(a) the format and frequency of reports submitted under this Act;

(b) procedures for investigation and hearings;

(c) the operation of the Ethnic Equity Register and public dashboard;

(d) complaint handling mechanisms and confidentiality safeguards;

(e) penalties for continuing non-compliance; and

(f) any other matter necessary for the implementation of this Act.

Transitional provisions.

(1) Within six months of the commencement of this Act, all public institutions shall —

(a) submit baseline data on ethnic composition as at the effective date;

(b) align their reporting formats with the templates issued by the Commission;

(c) designate an officer responsible for equity reporting and compliance.

(2) The Commission shall publish an inaugural National Ethnic Equity Benchmark Report within twelve months of this Act’s commencement.
