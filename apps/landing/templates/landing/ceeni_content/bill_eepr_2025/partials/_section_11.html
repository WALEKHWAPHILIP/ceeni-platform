11. Fourth Schedule — Consequential Amendments

(Section 38)

1. Amendment to the Public Service Commission Act

(1) The Public Service Commission Act is amended by inserting the following new section immediately after the relevant section to be identified by the drafters—

“Compliance with the Ethnic Equity and Public Representation Act

(a) The Commission shall, in all recruitment, promotion, and appointment decisions, comply with the provisions of the Ethnic Equity and Public Representation Act, 2025.

(b) The Commission shall submit quarterly workforce composition reports to the Commission for Ethnic Equity and National Inclusion (CEENI) in the prescribed format.

(c) Failure to comply with this section shall constitute grounds for administrative sanctions under both this Act and the Ethnic Equity and Public Representation Act, 2025.”

2. Amendment to the County Governments Act

(1) The County Governments Act is amended by inserting the following new section in Part VII—

“County Public Service Boards — Equity Compliance

(a) Each County Public Service Board shall ensure ethnic equity in county employment and submit quarterly compliance reports to the Commission for Ethnic Equity and National Inclusion (CEENI).

(b) The Board shall ensure that no single ethnic group exceeds 35% of the county public workforce unless otherwise authorized under the Ethnic Equity and Public Representation Act, 2025.

(c) Failure to comply shall attract administrative measures including budgetary sanctions, hiring freezes, or referral to relevant oversight bodies.”

3. Amendment to the State Corporations Act

(1) The State Corporations Act is amended by inserting the following new provision—

“Equity Monitoring and Reporting Obligations

(a) Every State Corporation shall include ethnic equity compliance as a performance indicator in the annual performance contract of its Chief Executive Officer.

(b) Each State Corporation shall designate a senior officer responsible for equity compliance and data reporting to CEENI.

(c) Non-compliance shall be subject to administrative review and may result in removal from office, budgetary restrictions, or dissolution.”
