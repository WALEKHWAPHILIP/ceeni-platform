3. Part III — The Commission (CEENI)

Establishment of the Commission.

There is established a body to be known as the Commission for Ethnic Equity and National Inclusion, which shall be a body corporate with perpetual succession and a common seal and shall, in its corporate name, be capable of —

(a) suing and being sued;

(b) taking, purchasing or otherwise acquiring, holding, charging or disposing of movable and immovable property;

(c) entering into contracts; and

(d) doing or performing all such other acts or things necessary for the proper performance of its functions under this Act which may lawfully be done or performed by a body corporate.

Composition of the Commission.

(1) The Commission shall consist of —

(a) a Chairperson, who shall be a person of high moral character and integrity, and with experience in public administration, human rights, or law;

(b) six other members with expertise in the areas of public service, data analysis, law, minority rights, or public finance;

(c) the Chief Executive Officer, appointed under section 10, who shall serve as secretary to the Commission.

(2) The Chairperson and members shall be appointed through a transparent and competitive process by the President, subject to approval by the National Assembly.

(3) In appointing members, the appointing authority shall ensure —

(a) gender parity;

(b) representation of marginalized and minority communities; and

(c) regional and ethnic balance.

Functions of the Commission.

The functions of the Commission shall be to —

(a) monitor, evaluate, and report on the ethnic composition of public institutions in Kenya;

(b) develop and publish national benchmarks for ethnic equity in public appointments and service;

(c) receive and investigate complaints related to ethnic exclusion or bias in public service;

(d) issue compliance reports and equity scorecards for each public institution annually;

(e) advise the national and county governments on measures to promote ethnic equity and inclusion;

(f) establish and maintain an Ethnic Equity Register;

(g) conduct public awareness and civic education on the right to equitable representation;

(h) exercise such other functions as may be conferred by this Act or any other law.

Powers of the Commission.

In the performance of its functions, the Commission shall have power to —

(a) access any public data, records, or statistics relevant to its mandate;

(b) summon any person to appear before it or produce any relevant document;

(c) recommend sanctions, corrective actions, or policy reforms to relevant authorities;

(d) collaborate with other constitutional commissions and public bodies;

(e) make rules for the conduct of its operations and internal governance.

Independence of the Commission.

Except as expressly provided by this Act or the Constitution, the Commission shall, in the exercise of its functions, be independent and shall not be subject to the direction or control of any person or authority.
