6. Part VI — Public Participation

Right to information.

Every Kenyan has the right to access information relating to ethnic representation in public institutions, including equity reports, scorecards, and compliance data published by the Commission.

Transparency in appointments.

(1) All public institutions shall publish, on their official platforms, information relating to —

(a) available positions and their requirements;

(b) selection and vetting processes; and

(c) final appointments, including names and ethnic backgrounds of appointees.

(2) The Commission shall monitor such disclosures and may issue notices for non-compliance.

Equity awareness and civic education.

(1) The Commission shall conduct regular civic education campaigns to inform citizens about their rights under this Act, how to participate, and how to report exclusion.

(2) Such campaigns shall be conducted in a manner that reaches diverse communities and uses accessible language, formats, and channels.

Citizen reporting portal.

The Commission shall operate a secure, anonymous portal where individuals can —

(a) report cases of bias, underrepresentation, or exclusion;

(b) submit testimonials, data, or concerns; and

(c) track institutional performance over time.

Stakeholder engagement.

(1) The Commission shall, at least once every year, convene stakeholder forums to —

(a) review progress in ethnic equity implementation;

(b) gather feedback from citizens, civil society, and experts; and

(c) update benchmarks and strategic plans accordingly.

(2) Reports from such forums shall be published and submitted to Parliament and the Council of Governors.
