5. Part V — Complaints and Enforcement

Right to lodge a complaint.

(1) Any person who alleges ethnic exclusion, bias, or unfair treatment in a public appointment or institution may lodge a complaint with the Commission.

(2) Complaints may be filed —

(a) individually or on behalf of a group or community;

(b) anonymously or with identifying details; and

(c) physically, electronically, or through other accessible means prescribed by the Commission.

Preliminary review.

(1) Upon receiving a complaint, the Commission shall conduct a preliminary assessment to determine admissibility within fourteen days.

(2) Where a complaint is found inadmissible, the complainant shall be informed in writing with reasons.

Investigation and findings.

(1) If the complaint is admissible, the Commission shall initiate an investigation and may request relevant records, summon witnesses, or inspect premises.

(2) Investigations shall be concluded within sixty days, and a determination issued in writing.

(3) The Commission may —

(a) recommend corrective measures;

(b) issue a compliance directive; or

(c) refer the matter to the CEENI Tribunal for hearing and enforcement.

CEENI Tribunal.

(1) There is established a tribunal to be known as the CEENI Tribunal which shall hear and determine cases referred to it under this Act.

(2) The composition, jurisdiction, and procedure of the Tribunal shall be as provided in the Third Schedule.

Enforceability of rulings.

(1) A ruling of the Commission or the CEENI Tribunal shall be binding and enforceable against any public institution.

(2) Where a public institution fails to comply, the Commission may —

(a) recommend administrative sanctions;

(b) petition Parliament or county assembly for oversight action; or

(c) institute proceedings in a court of competent jurisdiction.

Protection of whistleblowers.

No person shall be penalized, victimized, or harassed for lodging a complaint or providing information under this Act. The Commission shall put in place appropriate protective measures for whistleblowers.
