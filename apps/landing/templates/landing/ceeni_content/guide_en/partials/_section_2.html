2. The Problem

Right now, public jobs in Kenya are not shared fairly among all communities.

Instead of following clear rules, appointments often depend on:

  • Political deals made after elections.
  • Ethnic loyalty, where leaders favour people from their own community.
  • Patronage systems — “if we win, our people eat.”

This means some communities get more than their fair share of positions, while others are left out completely.

The result?

  • Good people are overlooked just because they come from the “wrong” tribe.
  • Some communities lose trust in government because they feel excluded.
  • Services suffer because jobs are not always given to the most qualified people.

Kenya’s 2010 Constitution calls for inclusivity and fairness in public service — but without a clear formula and strong enforcement, these values remain only words on paper.
