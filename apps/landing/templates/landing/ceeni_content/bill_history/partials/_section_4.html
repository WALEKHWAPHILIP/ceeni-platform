4. Impact on Public Institutions and Service Delivery

The politicisation of ethnicity has direct, measurable effects on how Kenyaâ€™s public sector is staffed, how it performs, and how citizens perceive its fairness.

4.1 Distorted Recruitment and Promotion

  • Merit is diluted, lowering capability in technical and leadership roles.
  • Career pathways skew toward those with political proximity.
  • HR data becomes unreliable due to under-reporting or reclassification.

4.2 Institutional Capture and Policy Drift

  • Boards and management align with factions, not the public interest.
  • Coalition needs dictate policies, not service delivery outcomes.
  • Leaders optimise for short-term cycles rather than long-term goals.

4.3 Inequitable Service Footprints

  • Uneven allocation of projects and staffing across regions.
  • Minority communities face barriers to accessing services.
  • Trust deficits emerge in areas excluded from decision-making.

4.4 Fiscal Inefficiencies

  • Bloated wage bills and duplicated roles.
  • Weakened procurement oversight increases risks.
  • Corrective litigation and audits drain public resources.

4.5 Erosion of Rule-of-Law Norms

  • Legal requirements are treated as suggestions, not mandates.
  • Whistleblowers are unprotected, silencing internal accountability.

4.6 Public Trust and Cohesion

  • Hiring perceived as unfair breeds cynicism and resentment.
  • Citizens disengage from governance.

4.7 Why the EEPR Bill Changes This

  • Introduces binding audits, data standards, and compliance orders.
  • Mandates public transparency via dashboards and scorecards.
  • Replaces coalition bargaining with algorithmic proportionality.
  • Empowers citizen feedback through structured participation loops.
