Why This Bill Matters

Ethnic Equity and Public Representation Bill (EEPR Bill, 2025)

These are the nine civic narratives that make the EEPR Bill not just a law - but a national turning point.

{% include 'common/buttons/_action_btn_read_bill.html' %}

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_section_header.html' %} The 9 Civic Transformations at the Heart of the EEPR Bill

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_tribal_fairness.html' %}

From Tribal Capture to National Fairness

This Bill ends the idea that government is a prize for the tribe in power. It redefines public service as a national trust — where all communities are included, respected, and protected under law.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_fear_to_issues.html' %}

From Fear-Based Politics to Issue-Based Campaigns

With CEENI in place, tribal incitement becomes pointless. Politicians must now talk about jobs, schools, and healthcare — not ethnicity.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_turn_to_belong.html' %}

From “Our Turn to Eat” to “Everyone’s Turn to Belong”

This Bill guarantees small tribes a seat at the table. Big tribes still qualify — but based on merit, not tribal loyalty.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_data_equity.html' %}

From Cosmetic Diversity to Data-Driven Equity

CEENI introduces annual scorecards and public dashboards. Equity is no longer a vague promise — it’s measurable and enforceable.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_unity_to_inclusion.html' %}

From Talk About Unity to Enforced Inclusion

Article 10 of our Constitution promised fairness. The EEPR Bill makes that promise real — with audits, sanctions, and timelines.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_hate_to_hope.html' %}

From Hate Speech to Hope Speech

Politicians who can’t deliver services often stoke ethnic fears. This Bill removes public office as a tribal prize — silencing hate at its root.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_cynicism_to_empowerment.html' %}

From Voter Cynicism to Civic Empowerment

Citizens will report violations, track compliance, and shape equity benchmarks through CEENI’s dashboard and mobile feedback tools.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_youth_to_justice.html' %}

From Youth Disillusionment to Generational Justice

Young Kenyans want merit, not last names. This law levels the field — so talent finally outranks tribe.

{% include 'common/icons/_icon_ethnic_to_accountability.html' %}

From Ethnic Arithmetic to Institutional Accountability

The EEPR Bill forces every ministry, county, and agency to report — and fix — ethnic imbalances. No more guessing. No more excuses.
